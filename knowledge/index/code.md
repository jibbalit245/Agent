## Foundational Textbooks (Free/Open Access)

### Algorithms & Data Structures
- **CLRS — Introduction to Algorithms** (Cormen et al., 4th ed)
  https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262046305/introduction-to-algorithms/
  The definitive reference. MIT OpenCourseWare has lecture videos.

- **The Algorithm Design Manual** (Skiena)
  https://www.algorist.com/
  Practical design techniques, war stories. Companion site has code.

- **Competitive Programmer's Handbook** (Laaksonen — free PDF)
  https://cses.fi/book/book.pdf
  Concise, implementation-focused coverage of contest-level algorithms.

- **NIST Dictionary of Algorithms and Data Structures**
  https://xlinux.nist.gov/dads/
  Authoritative definitions with complexity bounds.

### Programming Language Theory
- **TAPL — Types and Programming Languages** (Pierce)
  https://www.cis.upenn.edu/~bcpierce/tapl/
  The standard PLT reference. Lambda calculus through advanced type systems.

- **SICP — Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs** (Abelson & Sussman)
  https://web.mit.edu/6.001/6.037/sicp.pdf  |  https://mitp-content-server.mit.edu/books/content/sectbyfn/books_pres_0/6515/sicp.zip
  Foundational CS thinking. Also at https://sarabander.github.io/sicp/

- **Crafting Interpreters** (Nystrom — free)
  https://craftinginterpreters.com/
  Build two interpreters from scratch. Exceptional clarity.

### Systems & Distributed
- **Designing Data-Intensive Applications** (Kleppmann)
  https://dataintensive.net/
  Databases, streams, consensus. The best modern systems book.

- **Operating Systems: Three Easy Pieces** (Arpaci-Dusseau — free)
  https://pages.cs.wisc.edu/~remzi/OSTEP/
  Virtualization, concurrency, persistence. Free online.

- **Computer Networking: A Top-Down Approach** (Kurose & Ross)
  https://gaia.cs.umass.edu/kurose_ross/
  Supplemental slides and labs freely available.

- **The Art of Computer Programming** (Knuth — TAOCP)
  https://cs.stanford.edu/~knuth/taocp.html
  Volumes 1-4B. The authoritative mathematical CS reference.

### Machine Learning & AI
- **Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning** (Bishop — free PDF)
  https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/publication/pattern-recognition-machine-learning/
  The classical ML theory reference. Bayesian perspective.

- **Deep Learning** (Goodfellow, Bengio, Courville — free)
  https://www.deeplearningbook.org/
  Foundations of neural networks and deep architectures.

- **Mathematics for Machine Learning** (Deisenroth et al — free)
  https://mml-book.github.io/
  Linear algebra, probability, optimization for ML.

- **Probabilistic Machine Learning** (Murphy — free)
  https://probml.github.io/pml-book/
  Volumes 1 & 2. Bayesian and probabilistic ML at graduate level.

## Research Venues & Preprint Servers

### Preprints (Open Access)
- **arXiv Computer Science** — https://arxiv.org/list/cs/recent
  Subsections: cs.AI, cs.LG (ML), cs.CV, cs.CL (NLP), cs.DS, cs.PL, cs.DC, cs.CR
- **Semantic Scholar** — https://www.semanticscholar.org/
  AI-powered search across 200M+ papers. Has open API.
- **Papers With Code** — https://paperswithcode.com/
  ML papers + reproducible code. Benchmark leaderboards.

### Peer-Reviewed Journals & Conferences
- **ACM Digital Library** — https://dl.acm.org/
  STOC, FOCS, POPL, PLDI, SOSP, OSDI, NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR (in ACM/IEEE)
- **IEEE Xplore** — https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/
  CVPR, ICCV, IROS, software engineering, circuits
- **JMLR** (open access) — https://jmlr.org/
  Journal of Machine Learning Research. Full open access.
- **PLOS Computational Biology** — https://journals.plos.org/ploscompbiol/
  Open access computational methods

## Tools & APIs for Programmatic Research

- **Semantic Scholar API** — https://api.semanticscholar.org/
  Paper search, author graphs, citation networks. Free, no key needed (basic).
- **CrossRef API** — https://api.crossref.org/
  DOI resolution, citation data, works metadata.
- **arXiv API** — https://arxiv.org/help/api/
  Search and retrieve papers programmatically.
- **GitHub Code Search** — https://cs.github.com/
  Search actual implementation code. Also via GitHub API.
- **SourceGraph** — https://sourcegraph.com/search
  Cross-repository code search at scale.
