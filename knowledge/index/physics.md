## Foundational References (Free/Open Access)

### The Feynman Lectures (Free Online — Caltech)
https://www.feynmanlectures.caltech.edu/
- **Vol I** — Mechanics, Radiation, Heat: /I_toc.html
- **Vol II** — Electromagnetism, Matter: /II_toc.html
- **Vol III** — Quantum Mechanics: /III_toc.html
The most insightful introductory physics text ever written. Essential reading.

### Classical Mechanics
- **Goldstein, Poole & Safko** — Classical Mechanics (graduate standard)
  Lagrangian/Hamiltonian mechanics, canonical transformations, chaos
- **Taylor** — Classical Mechanics (clearer undergraduate→grad)
- **Morin** — Introduction to Classical Mechanics (Harvard, problems focused)
  https://scholar.harvard.edu/morin/home (problem sets available)

### Quantum Mechanics
- **Griffiths** — Introduction to Quantum Mechanics
  Standard first-year grad text. Clearest exposition.
- **Shankar** — Principles of Quantum Mechanics
  Mathematical rigour, Dirac notation, path integrals
- **Cohen-Tannoudji** (2 vols) — Quantum Mechanics
  Most complete QM reference at advanced undergrad/grad level
- **Sakurai & Napolitano** — Modern Quantum Mechanics
  Graduate standard. Angular momentum, perturbation theory, scattering.
- **Weinberg** — Lectures on Quantum Mechanics
  Modern perspective from Nobel laureate

### General & Special Relativity
- **Carroll — Spacetime and Geometry** (free lecture notes version)
  https://arxiv.org/abs/gr-qc/9712019
  Best accessible intro to GR. Full book: https://www.preposterousuniverse.com/spacetimeandgeometry/
- **Misner, Thorne & Wheeler** — Gravitation (MTW)
  The encyclopedic GR reference. 1279 pages.
- **Wald** — General Relativity
  Mathematical treatment, graduate level
- **Taylor & Wheeler** — Spacetime Physics (SR)
  The best special relativity introductory text.

### Electrodynamics
- **Griffiths** — Introduction to Electrodynamics
  Undergraduate-graduate bridge. Exceptionally clear.
- **Jackson** — Classical Electrodynamics
  Graduate standard. Comprehensive, mathematically demanding.
- **Zangwill** — Modern Electrodynamics (Cambridge)
  More modern treatment than Jackson.

### Statistical Mechanics & Thermodynamics
- **Pathria & Beale** — Statistical Mechanics
  Graduate standard. Ensembles, phase transitions, critical phenomena.
- **Kardar** — Statistical Physics of Particles + Fields (free MIT OCW)
  https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/8-333-statistical-mechanics-i-statistical-mechanics-of-particles-fall-2013/
- **Landau & Lifshitz** — Statistical Physics
  Part of the Course of Theoretical Physics series (10 vols, all essential)
- **Callen** — Thermodynamics and an Introduction to Thermostatistics

### Condensed Matter
- **Ashcroft & Mermin** — Solid State Physics
  The definitive reference for condensed matter.
- **Kittel** — Introduction to Solid State Physics
  Standard undergraduate condensed matter
- **Anderson** — Basic Notions of Condensed Matter Physics

### Quantum Field Theory
- **Peskin & Schroeder** — Introduction to Quantum Field Theory
  The graduate standard. Feynman diagrams, renormalization.
- **Weinberg** — The Quantum Theory of Fields (3 vols)
  Most complete QFT reference.
- **Srednicki** — Quantum Field Theory (free PDF)
  https://web.physics.ucsb.edu/~mark/qft.html
  Very readable, modern notation.

## Data & Reference Databases

- **NIST Physical Constants** — https://physics.nist.gov/cuu/Constants/
  Authoritative values. Downloadable in ASCII/JSON.
- **NIST Atomic Spectra Database** — https://physics.nist.gov/PhysRefData/ASD/lines_form.html
- **Particle Data Group (PDG)** — https://pdg.lbl.gov/
  All particle properties, reviews of particle physics. Free annual update.
- **NIST Chemistry WebBook** — https://webbook.nist.gov/
  Thermochemical, spectroscopic, reaction data
- **HyperPhysics** — https://hyperphysics.phy-astr.gsu.edu/hbase/hframe.html
  Concept-map navigation. Great for quick formula lookups.
